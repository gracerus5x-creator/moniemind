## Why Moniemind exists

Nigerian personal finance content is dominated by two extremes: (1) breathless promotion of whichever fintech app is currently advertising hardest, or (2) generic "save more, invest more" advice that ignores the realities of Naira inflation, FX access, IATA / BDC dynamics, and the patchwork of Nigerian regulation.

**Moniemind** sits between them. We cover the apps, accounts, and strategies that actually work for Nigerian professionals — with honest cost breakdowns, regulatory context, and the trade-offs nobody else mentions.

## Who we are

**Moniemind Desk** is an independent editorial team covering personal finance for Nigerian professionals. Based in Lagos, with collective experience navigating Nigerian fintech, dollar access, and remote-income realities through multiple FX regimes.

We are **not** financial advisors, regulated brokers, or licensed wealth managers. Moniemind is independent journalism plus practical guides — **not personalized financial advice**.

## What we cover

- **Investment apps for Nigerians** — Bamboo, Risevest, Cowrywise, Trove, PiggyVest: real cost comparisons, hidden fees, withdrawal timelines, and which fits which use case
- **Dollar access** — domiciliary accounts, P2P, IATA / BDC realities, fintech swaps
- **NGX equities and government securities** — how to buy, what to watch, what to avoid
- **US-market access from Nigeria** — Alpaca, Webull alternatives, IB Nigeria options
- **Practical Naira topics** — savings strategies that work despite inflation, side-income channels, regulatory shifts (SEC Nigeria, CBN)

## Our editorial standards

1. **Naira amounts paired with USD** wherever it matters — exchange rates move; we name our rate-as-of date.
2. **Regulatory context included** — when SEC Nigeria, CBN, or NFIU has spoken on something, we link the primary source.
3. **No guaranteed returns.** Anyone promising one is selling something. We call those out.
4. **Platform reviews include the bad** — bad withdrawal experiences, downtime, customer-service problems. If a platform is good with caveats, the caveats stay in the article.
5. **Dated reviews.** Nigerian fintech moves fast — new fees, new partnerships, regulatory shifts. We re-verify articles quarterly and add dated correction notes when reality changes.

## Editorial independence

- We accept **affiliate / referral commissions** from some platforms — fully disclosed on our [Affiliate Disclosure](/disclosure/) page. Specific direct-affiliate partners include Bamboo, Risevest, and others.
- We do **not** accept payment to write positive coverage.
- We do **not** hide negative platform experiences because the platform pays a commission.
- We are willing to recommend non-affiliate options (e.g. direct NGX broker accounts) when those are the better choice.
