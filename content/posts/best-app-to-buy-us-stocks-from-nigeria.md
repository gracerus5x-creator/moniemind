## Why Buying US Stocks From Nigeria Is Still Complicated (But Getting Better)

Let's not sugarcoat it. The CBN's FX restrictions, the gap between official rates and BDC rates, and the PTA/BTA dollar limits (currently capped at $4,000 and $5,000 per quarter respectively) mean that funding a foreign brokerage account is not as simple as transferring from your GTBank app.

Most Nigerians can't just wire funds to a US broker like Interactive Brokers or Charles Schwab without hitting compliance walls. That's exactly why local fintech platforms that handle the FX routing on your behalf have become essential infrastructure.

According to [SEC Nigeria's 2024 capital-markets guidance](https://www.sec.gov.ng), investments in foreign securities by Nigerian residents must go through SEC-registered investment platforms or licensed dealing members — which narrows your legitimate options considerably.

## What to Check Before You Fund Any App

1. **SEC Nigeria registration** — Verify on [sec.gov.ng](https://www.sec.gov.ng) before depositing
2. **FX rate transparency** — Ask or test what naira-to-dollar rate they apply
3. **Withdrawal timelines** — Knowing how long it takes to get your money back matters
4. **Custody arrangement** — Who holds your shares? (DriveWealth, Apex Clearing, etc.)
5. **Tax obligations** — US stock gains may have Nigerian tax implications; consult a local tax professional
